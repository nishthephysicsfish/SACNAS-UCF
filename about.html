About SACNAS

The Society for Advancement of Chicanos/Hispanics & Native Americans in Science (SACNAS) is a national organization dedicated to fostering the success of Hispanic/Chicano and Native American scientists — from college students to professionals — to advance science and community. SACNAS promotes diversity and inclusion within STEM fields by providing resources, mentorship, networking, and opportunities for underrepresented individuals. Learn more about SACNAS at www.sacnas.org.

SACNAS @ UCF Mission & Events

Our chapter, SACNAS @ UCF, is committed to empowering students through programming that supports their academic and professional growth in STEM. Our mission focuses on creating a welcoming and inclusive environment for diverse communities at UCF to thrive.

Here are some materia we plan to offer:

  • Workshops on graduate school and summer research program applications
  • Guidance on applying for scholarships
  • Support for getting involved in research and discovering your field of interest
  • Tips on communicating your research effectively, both verbally and in writing
  • Discussions on why participating in research and pursuing graduate school can be valuable
